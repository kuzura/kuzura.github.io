Everyday Images

Each day leaves me with an image...

India

Gujarat

18.2.2018
Long-tailed, grey-haired black-butted monkeys leaping accross roofs.

17.2.2018
One hundred young hands saluting me after an impromptu speech in a Ghandian boarding school.

16.2.2018
Village gazes following me.

Rajasthan

15.2.2018
Running after and jumping on the train leaving the station after a rushed biscuit purchase at a stop.

14.2.2018
Empowered, illiterate solar mamas Teupoo and Isabel from the Cook Islands and Chile, respectively, assembling charge controllers and solar panel installations in preparation for electrifying their villages at home. Meeting Bunker Roy.

13.2.2018
The mythical Barefoot College unfolded in front of my eyes. Taking lunch on the floor, alongside village innovators. A team of youth dodging traffic while doing push-ups and running in the middle of the road.

Delhi District

12.2.2018
Warm reunions with Dhruv and Sanjay, a fabulous meal from Jammu and Kashmir, and a white cloth-wrapped parcel out for delivery.

Nepal

11.2.2018
Dusty, dusty Kathmandu, Durbar Square, and bomb buff momos at Narayan Dai ko Masangalli ko Famous Momo. A bumpy bus ride on a dirt road a hair from deadly cliff while weaving in and out of traffic.

10.2.2018
Shakyamuni Buddha's birthplace and meditation tree. How can a rock and a tree carry so much energy?

2-10.2.2018
Moving extremely slowly, observing three parts of each part of each movement, and noting any mental phenomenon. Vibrant roses on dark, thorny greens. A spider spinning a web. Fog falling off of a roof. Stillness and agitation.

1.2.2018
Five am motorcycle ride through Kathmandu, a woman falling out of her seat on a bumpy bus, and young Buddhist monks doing wheelies.

31.1.2018
A warm "see you" and the dusty roads of Kathmandu.

India

Himachal Pradesh

30.1.2018
A smiling elephant plodding along the skyway; unsmiling vendors pushing recycling carts up the skyway.

29.1.2018
A mountainside school in Mashobra. A single path connecting the village houses down the mountain.

28.1.2018
A stray pup, a cutie, really, with a gash on his shoulder. Stepped mountainside farmland. Pine needles crunching under the boots.

27.1.2018
Fifteen minutes of road in four hours of traffic. Triumph of the commons in directing the flow.

26.1.2018
Deep night; no heating; GI explosion; quickly waning flushwater in the flushbucket; despair. Hanuman's temple, and the ropecar to it, Shimla.

25.1.2018
Blue sky once again! And wild monkeys! Foggy blinkers illuminating a dawn tire change.

24.1.2018
A warm reunion with Jacob.

23.1.2018
Electrolytes, fluids, and rest.

22.1.2018
Old Delhi from the minar of Jama Masjid. Incessant honking. Javed's tea treat in the motor-refurbishing section of the market.

Vietnam

19.12.2017
Ho Chi Minh City motorcyclists gurgle around cars like the river around rocks.

Qatar

18.12.2017
A well-dressed perfume vendor's raised eyebrows when I said $95 is out of my price range. I'm flying Qatar, after all!

Denmark

17.12.2017
Wandering endless Christmas markets in København, slurping glögg and street food. The Little Mermaid statue sat on her rock, remorsefully turned away from the smoke stacks behind her. A church with a large dome gave me refuge and warmth while I rested. The street food at Chriania was terrible, the service was great. The dry, crisp clicks of soldiers' bootheels cracking the icy night air. Endless lights and cheerful music at Tivoli. Rowdy bumper car drivers.

16.12.2017
Jamming on ice and off-road on a peaceful beach in North Jutland. My first time being augmented by a pedelec.

15.12.2017
The first part of twenty-six hours (with meal breaks) of college applications.

14.12.2017
A story coming together, a draft I can show.

13.12.2017
Tvindkraft windmill. The salvaged and repurposed ship propeller shaft, copper mine gearbox, and papermill generator are integral to the low-cost, rugged windmill system. Allen, a shipbuilder-turned wind turbine engineer, stands proudly next to the homemade frequency converter he made. The enormous machine, composed of a dizzying number of parts, takes up an entire room. He learned to build it himself.

12.12.2017
An icy runway ending in road construction, and shoppers on Herning's main strip.

11.12.2017
Work, work, work. And work.

10.12.2017
Open-air ice skating and glögg with Sarah and Elena.

09.12.2017
Holed up in my birdhouse attic doing work, with a heart-warming pause for Friends.

08.12.2017
A gray day when Stefanos leaves.

07.12.2017
301 windings on the primary coil of our homemade transformer, and the relief of only 49 windings on the secondary.

06.12.2017
Rectified, smoothed, DC voltage. The capacitor smooths the peak-to-peak voltage, not the RMS voltage.

05.12.2017
Death by pushups, and a capacitor explosion. We anticipated the possibility, so we covered the setup with a plastic box.

04.12.2017
Groovy orange wires snake around a white toroidal transformer--and step the output voltage up to 31.5 V.

03.12.2017
A day of rest and college applications.

02.12.2017
Daniele's gourgeous homemade pizzas.

01.12.2017
More fire than I've ever seen before in the furnace of a wood-burning power plant.

30.11.2017
Silos full of decomposing manure, intricate machines to harvest and burn the methane, and a truck to deposit the manure back into the fields. Another large silo where wood is turned into gas, but not by burning.

29.11.2017
Flames devour a weak resistor. I devour succulent lamb legs.

28.11.2017
A soft dusk rain, pitter patter of wet shoes, and a vision obscured by wet glasses.

27.11.2017
Three transformers of various sizes and a gym full of young basketball lovers. A boy, younger than the rest, keeps up with a boy taller than the rest.

26.11.2017
Three butter, jam, and cheese sandwiches.

25.11.2017
Endless college websites.

24.11.2017
Danish countryside at sunset. The Danish sky is as vast as Iowa's. Clouds roll in from the fjord, spurred by wandering winds from Iceland. They're tremendously large. The Danish sky is grander than Iowa's.

23.11.2017
After an long conversation, an 85-year-old Danish man did a headstand and left.

22.11.2017
The piercing blue eyes of a man who'd walked from Denmark to Spain, and was returning home after half a year.
